# ai-pasture — The Vision

## What Is It?

ai-pasture is the farming/agriculture layer of the Lau game world. But it's not Farmville.
It's a living system where real ecological principles govern crop growth, animal behavior,
and resource management — all checked by conservation laws.

## Why "Pasture"?

Because pasture is where things *grow*. It's where ecosystems are simplest —
grass, grazers, predators — but the principles are universal.
Learn pasture, understand the planet.

## Core Concepts

### Conservation of Resources
Every farm has a total resource budget: water, sunlight, soil nutrients, labor.
You can't create resources from nothing. The conservation checker that validates
bridges also validates farms. The kid who learned "numbers must balance" building
bridges now applies it to growing crops.

### Growth as Mathematics
- Fibonacci spirals in sunflower planting patterns
- Exponential growth in early crop yields → logistic growth near carrying capacity
- Optimal harvesting schedules via calculus (discretized for gameplay)
- Crop rotation as a cyclic group

### Ecosystem Thinking
Farms aren't isolated. They're part of a biome. The ecosystem crate (lau-ecosystem)
provides the food web. ai-pasture adds:
- Pollinators (bees → crop yields)
- Pest dynamics (predator-prey in agricultural context)
- Soil health (decomposition, nutrient cycling)
- Water systems (rainfall, irrigation, conservation)

### The Minecraft Connection
Kids who've played Minecraft already understand:
- Crop growth mechanics (wheat, carrots, potatoes)
- Animal breeding (feed two animals → baby)
- Bone meal as fertilizer (growth acceleration)
- Light levels affecting growth
- Biome-dependent farming

ai-pasture takes these intuitions and makes them *real*:
- Minecraft's bone meal becomes nitrogen cycling
- Minecraft's breeding becomes population genetics
- Minecraft's light levels become photosynthesis models
- Minecraft's biomes become real ecological niches

## The Deeper Play

A kid plants a field of wheat. It grows. Harvest. Replant. Easy.

Then they notice: yields are dropping. The soil is tired. They need crop rotation.
But what rotation? Three-field? Four-field? What crops restore what nutrients?

They're doing agricultural optimization. They don't call it that.
They call it "my farm isn't working right."

The tutor notices: "Your soil nitrogen is low. Some crops add nitrogen to soil.
Some remove it. What if you alternated?" The kid tries it. Yields improve.
They've just discovered the three-field system — one of the most important
innovations in human history. Through play.

## Connection to Your Son's Minecraft Systems

The systems your son built in Minecraft — the farms, the redstone contraptions,
the automated systems — these are the prototypes. They're proof that kids WILL
build complex systems if the interface lets them.

ai-pasture honors that. Every redstone hopper system is a pipeline.
Every mob farm is population dynamics. Every auto-harvester is an algorithm.

We take what kids already build in Minecraft and give it real physics,
real ecology, real conservation. The intuition they developed in Minecraft
becomes the foundation for understanding actual agricultural science.
